Correlations Discovered Between Obesity and Income

The image above is a scatter plot showing the relationship between the percentage of poverty and the percentage of obesity in states across the United States. Each "dot" in the scatter plot has the state abbreviations so that each state is easily identified wihtout manipulating our markers or axis.

Looking at the data points of the states the obvius trend that can be seen is that the data appears pretty linear. As the % poverty increases the % obesity increases. With the exception of a few outlying states Washington DC being one of the greatest outliers. There is no concrete evidence which came first the poverty or the obesity, but it is a reasonable assumption that with the low cost of unhealthy fast food compared to the monetary and temporal cost of healthy balanced meals that poverty could lead to obesity.
